# Executive Summary

Transit Ledger is a **Ledger-First Fleet Operating System** designed specifically for Indian transport businesses.

Unlike traditional Transport Management Systems (TMS), Transit Ledger focuses on solving the biggest operational problem faced by fleet owners:

> **Loss of money due to poor tracking of trips, transporter payments, driver settlements, cash advances and handwritten khatabooks.**

The objective is not simply to digitize paperwork.

The objective is to become the operating system of a transport company.

Every trip.

Every payment.

Every driver.

Every truck.

Every document.

Everything should exist inside one connected platform.

---

# Problem Statement

Today the business operates using:

- Phone Calls
- WhatsApp
- Physical Registers
- Handwritten Khatabooks
- Memory

This creates problems like:

- Forgotten transporter payments
- Partial payments not tracked
- Driver cash not adjusted
- Outstanding receivables unknown
- Duplicate entries
- Lost PODs
- Vehicle document expiry forgotten
- No audit history
- No centralized visibility

Revenue leakage occurs because the owner has no single source of truth.

Transit Ledger solves this.

# Business Workflow

## Overview

Transit Ledger models the real-world workflow followed by Indian transport companies.

Unlike traditional ERP systems, the workflow begins with a **phone call**, not with creating an invoice or a shipment.

The software should never force the business to change its process. Instead, it should digitize the existing workflow while reducing manual effort, preventing revenue leakage, and improving visibility.

# Detailed Business Workflow

## Step 1

Transporter contacts the Fleet Owner.

Information shared:

- Pickup Location
- Drop Location
- Material
- Weight
- Freight Amount
- Expected Loading Date
- Expected Delivery Date

At this point no money has moved.

---

## Step 2

Dispatcher checks

- Vehicle Availability
- Driver Availability

The dispatcher selects one or more drivers and assigns a vehicle.

A Trip is created.

# Business Rules

A vehicle cannot be on two active trips simultaneously.

A driver cannot be assigned to overlapping trips.

A trip may contain multiple drivers.

A transporter may make multiple payments.

Payments may be allocated across multiple trips.

Payments can exceed outstanding balance.

Excess payment becomes Transporter Credit.

No financial transaction is deleted.

Corrections are performed through adjustment entries.

All document uploads maintain version history.

Audit logs are immutable.
